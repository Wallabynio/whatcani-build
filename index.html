The practice

Founded on expertise, built for results.

whatcani.build was built by practitioners who saw too many good projects fail early — not because the sites were wrong, but because the planning advice arrived too late. Next year the practice marks ten years of putting that advice first.

Istvan Bakos
Principal — Planning & Feasibility

Istvan leads the planning and feasibility side of the practice, with a background spanning architecture and urban design before moving into planning strategy. He built whatcani.build around the conviction that good planning advice should arrive before the money is spent, not after, and works directly with developers and builders on zoning strategy, feasibility, and the policy arguments that get good projects through council.

Nathan Evans
Principal — Design & Approvals

Nathan brings the design-literate half of the practice — reading plans, sections, and massing fluently enough to catch planning issues while a scheme can still be changed cheaply. Client outcomes

What working with us looks like

"

We were three weeks from exchange on a corner site in Earlwood and needed to know whether the R3 zoning would actually carry four townhouses. The feasibility review came back in six business days: a realistic yield of three, not four, a clear read on the Canterbury-Bankstown DCP setbacks, and a DA risk profile our lender could work with. We bought at the right price because of it.

Our architect's first scheme for a dual occupancy in Ryde blew the 8.5-metre height control and fell short on landscaped area — and we only found out after paying for it. The pre-design brief reset the envelope against the Ryde LEP before the redesign, and the second scheme went through without a single amended plan. It saved us roughly $15,000 in abortive design fees.
