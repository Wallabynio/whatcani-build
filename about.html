whatcani.build was built by practitioners who saw too many good projects fail early — not because the sites were wrong, but because the planning advice arrived too late. We exist to fix that.

Our principals

Meet the team

The people leading the practice bring complementary expertise across planning strategy, urban design, feasibility, and the approvals process.

Istvan Bakos
Principal — Planning & Feasibility

Istvan leads the planning and feasibility side of the practice, with a background spanning architecture and urban design before moving into planning strategy. He has spent his career at the early, high-stakes point in a project — the decisions made before a single drawing is finalised — and built whatcani.build around the conviction that good planning advice should arrive before the money is spent, not after.

He works directly with developers and builders on zoning strategy, feasibility, and the policy arguments that get good projects through council. That means reading a site the way an assessment planner will — controls, context, and precedent together — and shaping the case for approval early, while there is still room to change course cheaply.

NSW Planning System Feasibility Strategy SEPP & LEP Policy Urban Design
Nathan Evans
Principal — Design & Approvals

Nathan brings the design-literate half of the practice — reading plans, sections, and massing fluently enough to catch planning issues while a scheme can still be changed cheaply. Before co-founding whatcani.build, he worked across residential and mixed-use projects managing the DA and CDC process end to end, from first lodgement through to determination.

He leads pre-design and approvals work, translating planning controls into briefs that architects can actually design to, and planning reports that councils can actually approve. That dual fluency — design on one side, planning policy on the other — is what keeps a project moving between the drawing board and the assessment desk without expensive rework in between.

What we don't take on
—Major EIS or environmental impact statements
—Large infrastructure approvals
—Complex social impact assessments
—Land and Environment Court representation
—Projects outside the NSW planning framework
